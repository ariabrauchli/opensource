Week 2

The documentation process is underway, but as it is the first week and we do not have a van, there is not much to document. Due to travel and doctor appointments, I have not had time to get into a workflow this week, which is when I work best. I have been able to manage quick bursts of work and will be able to focus more starting tomorrow. I have, however, found multiple other van instructables online, such as websites and (sorry Annie) Tik Tok. (It's good for something at least!)

There have been no files to save yet. This will be a weekly goal.

I have successfully organized the Google Drive. Everything is organized into folders. This successfully declutters everything and makes navigation easier.

I write all feedback down where it is most convenient at the time, which has been the notes app on my phone. Later, I review the feedback to prioritize suggestions that align with my project's goals, focusing on areas that will have the greatest impact. I also share feedback with my team to brainstorm effective solutions.

I learned that when I have inconsistencies in my schedule, such as a time zone change, it becomes a lot more difficult for me to keep track of my normal schedule and I end up getting off track and missing things. I am on a slight delay, but only by an hour or two. My mind wants to be on vacation mode as it usually is when I travel, but this is not a vacation. I have learned to set time aside each day to work on my assignments and make progress. My house in DC has a really nice fireplace that makes it easier to do so.

Week 3

I had a hectic week traveling for medical reasons, so I had to miss class. Over the weekend, Charlie and Sammy filled me in, and I tackled the work in a sprint rather than a marathon, which usually suits me well. I think I made a decent amount of progress. I also got stuck on an error that broke the whole website and ended up spending many hours on that. As of now, I have a very basic website design, but at least it looks better than it did without css. I have also added the links for the navigation bar, although there isn't much to add yet. I will be continuing to add substance to these pages weekly.

After missing class, Charlie filled me in and gave me feedback on what I've done and instructed me on what to work on next. This helped me understand what needed improvement and what to focus on next. This helps me prioritize my work.

I had a major setback this week. My whole website became inaccessible! After every change I made and pushed into the repository, I would refresh the website so I could see what it looked like. It often ended up being quite slow to see the results. It's taken up to 40 minutes of refreshing before I see any changes. I added CSS styling, and when it finally updated, I couldn't access the page. I had no idea what I had done wrong because I've added similar CSS styling many times before without this happening. I immediately deleted the CSS file, but 40 minutes later, it was still the same. I spent hours on google (some of my search history is below) trying to find a solution. I ended up trying three different things. I'd wait twenty minutes in between each solution, but it wasn't clear which trick finally did it. It was really unexpected for me because everything was going so smoothly beforehand. I ended up working on this for hours. I learned that debugging web issues, especially deployment-related ones, requires a lot of patience and troubleshooting across multiple areas. I hadn't anticipated how long it would take for my changes to reflect, and it was frustrating not knowing if my fixes were working.

Week 6

My original goal was to work on the website. I kept that vague on purpose because I honestly wasn't sure how much I'd be able to accomplish each week. I always try to do as much as I can, but sometimes I end up doing more than I planned, or I shift focus to something that feels more important in the moment. What I'm really aiming for is a final website that looks professional and thoughtfully designed, not something that feels like it was thrown together after one basic HTML class. To help make that happen, I enrolled in an online course to sharpen my skills and improve the overall design. I've also been working on optimizing the site for mobile, although that has been a slower process. Alongside that, I've been sketching out ideas for how I want the finished version to look.

As for how I've been responding to feedback, I had a good conversation with Charlie about some parts of the original site that we hadn't addressed yet. I mentioned a few tabs that still didn't have clear direction. We decided together that I should move toward a layout that keeps most of the content on a single page, with skip navigation to make things easier to access. He also pointed out that I should focus more on mobile usability, which I hadn't really considered until he brought it up. That feedback helped shift the way I think about the project, and I've made it a priority since then.

One of the most unexpected challenges recently has been getting sick. I thought I had the flu, but I tested negative for everything, including COVID. It feels like the bronchitis I had over winter break came back, and it's been really hard to keep my energy up. On top of that, a close friend was diagnosed with cancer, and I lost another friend entirely. It's been an overwhelming and emotional week. It feels like every semester comes with something major like this, but I'm learning how to stay grounded when things fall apart. In the past, I might have shut down completely and failed, but now I'm at least still here, still trying. I might turn things in late sometimes, but I'm learning how to push through instead of giving up. I've also realized that people have expectations, and if you don't meet them, it can lead to disappointment or low grades. That's a hard lesson to keep learning. It's difficult to communicate when I'm barely functioning and all I want to do is shut down, but I'm doing my best. Charlie suggested I go for a walk, and I did. It didn't fix everything, but it helped a little, and that was enough for that day.

Week 8

This week, my main goal was to make serious progress on the website, and I've been working really hard to get there. I signed up for an online HTML course to improve my skills. While the course wasn't exactly what I needed, I still learned a lot from it. I've been putting in long hours, staying up late, and even pulling a few all-nighters to keep building and refining the site. After several versions and a lot of experimenting, I'm finally starting to create something I actually feel proud of.

I made the decision early on to code the site manually instead of using a website builder like Wix. I want it to look polished and unique, not like a drag-and-drop template. That's made the process more challenging, but also more rewarding. The layout is coming together, the structure makes sense, and I've already optimized it for mobile. I've also started gathering everything I need to finish the site, including headshots, clean renders of the product, high-quality photos of the final build, and a link to a view-only Onshape file where users can purchase the cut file.

Feedback has played a big part in helping me shape the site. I asked several people about the layout and how many pages would make sense. Based on their input, I decided to keep it as a multi-page site. One page will focus on the product, while another will give background information about our project and process. That approach feels more balanced and user-friendly.

On a more personal note, this week has been emotionally intense. Mei Mei stopped eating again, and it felt like another death scare. It shook me up more than I expected, and balancing that emotional weight with all the work has been tough. Still, I've kept showing up and doing what I can. I'm learning how to keep moving forward when things feel uncertain and how to keep building even when it hurts.

Overall, I'm proud of the progress I've made. It hasn't been easy, but I've stayed committed and kept pushing through. The site is finally starting to reflect the vision I had when I started, and I'm excited to keep going and bring it fully to life.

Week 9

My goal for this week was to completely finish the website. I'm proud to say that I did it. All the content is in place, it's styled with CSS, and honestly, I think it looks pretty good. There's still room for polishing, but it's functional, presentable, and complete. To get it done, I pulled three all-nighters. I work best in long, intense sprints, and once I get into the zone, it's hard to stop. Each sprint lasted over 12 hours. I canceled all my plans, barely left my room, and just kept pushing through until it was done.

Time management was intense, but it worked for me. I wasn't able to spread the work out like I'd originally planned, but the all-or-nothing approach helped me lock in and make it happen. It wasn't sustainable long-term, but for this final push, it got the job done.

I also had to respond to some unexpected challenges. Charlie got COVID this week, which meant I had to learn his part of the presentation in one day while also finishing the website. We went through the slides together multiple times, and I took detailed notes on everything he said I missed. When it came time to present, I was able to include about 95 percent of what we had planned. That felt like a win.

This week taught me a lot about my own expectations. I went into this thinking building a website would be easy and fun, but I didn't realize how much I still didn't know. There are so many tools and frameworks, like Bootstrap and more advanced JavaScript, that I haven't fully learned yet. I do know a bit of JavaScript, but not enough to build exactly what I wanted. At times I felt pretty discouraged for overestimating my skills. It's frustrating to be a senior and still feel like there's so much left to learn.

Week 10

This week, my goals were to make the website secure and do some basic editing, like fixing typos and alignment issues. I'm happy to say I met both of those goals. I finally figured out how to secure the website, thanks to a lot of trial and error and help from online resources. It took hours, but nothing too extreme. I also went through and cleaned up text errors and spacing issues. The only thing I couldn't quite figure out was adjusting the margins of the contact box, which was more complicated than I expected.

Time management went fairly well. I paced things out over the week and didn't have to rely on a last-minute sprint. That felt like a step forward. Still, there's always more to learn and do. Even after completing what I set out to do, I realized how much room for improvement there still is.

After I met my goals, I met with Charlie, who shared that we need to shift the direction of the website to better match a slightly new vision for the project. That means I'll need to do a full revamp soon. It was a little overwhelming to hear that after I had just finished everything for the week, but I'm going to start working on those changes. We also received feedback that the product shouldn't be offered for free anymore. So now, instead of giving users direct access to an adaptable CAD file, we'll be requiring a contact form submission for purchase.

This week was a reminder that a project is never really done. Even when something feels finished, there's usually more to improve. I've been working hard to feel like I'm doing enough, and the idea of redoing the site right before my birthday is stressful. My best friend, who I haven't seen in years, is flying out this weekend, and I don't know if I'll have time to actually enjoy it. Weekends are usually the only time I have to really get things done, so I'm feeling the pressure. Still, I'm proud that I learned how to secure a site and that I've been sticking with this, even when it's hard. I'm doing my best to keep moving forward.

Week 11

With the help of Sammy, the website has now been completely redesigned to reflect a more intentional and professional structure. We've moved from a multi-tab layout to a consolidated, streamlined experience that is easier for users to navigate. Instead of offering free downloadable files, we now require visitors to fill out a contact form to request a purchase. This change helps us protect our intellectual property and aligns better with our long-term business model. I spent the week implementing this new layout, troubleshooting navigation inconsistencies, and testing the mobile view on various devices. I also began drafting updated website copy with a more cohesive tone. It's starting to feel like a real business now, not just a class project. I'm finally finding some satisfaction in how everything is coming together, even though it's been a long road.

Week 12

This week was about polishing and preparing. I fine-tuned the margins and spacing across the site and made sure all links are functional and redirect correctly. I coordinated with Charlie and Sammy to gather the latest renders and updated product images for the site. We also added a testimonial section and began preparing the site for public launch. It's surreal to see how far we've come. What started as a rough HTML skeleton is now a fully functioning, mobile-optimized, visually cohesive site that represents our capstone project. I've grown a lot, not just technically, but also in how I handle unexpected setbacks and feedback. There's still more to do, but the foundation is finally solid. I feel proud of what I've built.
